A calendar is an object in the form of a booklet in which the month,
day, day of the week, anniversaries, holidays, etc. are marked for each day of the year.
A calendar (冊曆) is a document that records the age of one year and the date.

Diary

A diary (日記) is an unofficial, private record in which
an individual records daily experiences, thoughts, feelings, etc., on a daily basis.
Usually, it is recorded on a daily basis, but it varies greatly depending on
the individualb who writes it, and since it is not a compulsory public record,
it can actually be a period or a month depending on the interval of writing.
Diaries of the past often have the characteristics of a journal and a diary,
unlike today, although it is usually called a journal to leave an official record for a special purpose.

Music

Music is the art of arranging sounds in time through
the elements of melody, harmony, rhythm, and timbre.
It is one of the universal cultural aspects of all human societies.
General definitions of music include common elements
such as pitch (which governs melody and harmony),
rhythm (and its associated concepts tempo, meter, and articulation),
dynamics (loudness and softness), and the sonic qualities of timbre and texture (which are sometimes termed the "color" of a musical sound).

Album

A photo album made of thick paper on which photos,.
calligraphy and paintings, stamps, and Christmas cards are pasted..
Albums were originally created for the purpose of long-term preservation
by organizing and pasting photos to prevent damage and loss.
There are many convenient albums in which photos can be inserted, removed,
or repositioned freely by covering the land with a special adhesive and inserting photos between them.
